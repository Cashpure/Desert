HISTORY OF THE SAHARA

The Sahara Desert is the world's largest hot desert, covering around 3.6 million square miles in North Africa. It is known for its extreme temperatures, reaching up to 136Â°F in some areas. Despite its harsh climate, the Sahara is home to a variety of flora and fauna, including camels, fennec foxes, and date palm trees. Ancient civilizations, such as the Egyptians and Berbers, have also thrived in the Sahara's oasis towns. Today, the Sahara attracts tourists who come to marvel at its stunning landscapes and experience the unique culture of its nomadic people.
